More Than Just a Team

About Us

Cumann Spóirt an Phobail was named by Aine Pollock, and was initally set up as a community sports group in the local area of Ballymurphy by Jim Donnelly, Seamy Colnon and Oliver Pollock, whose kids attended an Irish scoil in the Ballymurphy area. The scoil had little or no funding at the time so the group planned, and cycled to Donegal to raise funds for that bunscoil, as well as other Scoilana. This became an annual event and went on for ten years. At that time, there was increased and prolonged anti-social behaviour within the upper Springfield area.

Cumann Spóirt an Phobail asked, and were given a weekly slot at Bunscoil an Tsleibhe Dhuibh as a drop in for young men in the area to use as an intervention project. The group started a five-a-side drop-in, with James Lowerie & Joe Donnelly playing alongside some of the most marginalized young lads in the area. The word was not long spreading, and numbers grew. The lads wanted more though… they wanted to become a team. They asked Oliver to take on the project of starting a team for them and that they would help with it. This was a task new to everyone, but we applied, and were accepted into the Belfast & District League.

In that first season the team went unbeaten, winning the League & Cup. The following season, due to popularity, numbers started growing which presented us with a chance to start a 2nd team. Due to the commitment from everyone involved, we have now become a huge part of the community, presenting opportunities for people of all ages, male, female and for all abilities.
